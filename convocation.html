Convocation is a momentous event in a student's academic journey, marking the culmination of years of hard work and dedication. It is a formal ceremony where degrees and diplomas are awarded to graduates in the presence of faculty, family, and friends. The event typically includes inspiring speeches from esteemed guests, faculty members, and valedictorians, reflecting on the graduates' achievements and the bright future ahead. Graduates don their academic regalia, receive their certificates, and often participate in the traditional cap-toss, symbolizing the end of their studies and the beginning of new opportunities. Convocation is a time of celebration, reflection, and pride, honoring the accomplishments of the graduates and the support of those who helped them succeed.
